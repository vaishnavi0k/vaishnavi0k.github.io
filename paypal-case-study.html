Before the work

App Observations

Before writing anything, I spent time in the PayPal app to understand how it already speaks. These patterns informed every copy decision across all three challenges.

🤝
Collaborative language throughout — "we," "let's," and "us" appear consistently, making interactions feel shared rather than transactional.
✂️
Consistent use of contractions. "We'll," "you're," "it's" — the app never gets stiff or formal, even when dealing with serious subjects.
Aa
Sentence-case across labels, headers, and CTAs. No title case. Keeps the visual tone calm and readable, not shouty.
→
Clear, direct phrasing across every screen — no jargon, no hedging. The app trusts the user to understand plain language.
💬
Brief context or rationale before all primary actions. Users always know why they're being asked to do something before they do it.
✦
Voice: friendly, conversational, and empowering. Even error states feel like the app is on your side.
Challenge 01

Identity Verification for Large Transfers

A user wants to send over $2,000 to a friend. Local regulations require the app to verify their identity for transactions above this threshold — collecting their date of birth and SSN. The challenge: ask for sensitive information without alarming the user or breaking their trust mid-flow.

Flow options considered
1
Send money → Context screen → Form screen → Back to send money Chosen
2
Send money → Context + form on one screen → Back to send money
3
Send money → Context → Enter details + send in one step
I separated the explanation from the form to build trust before asking for sensitive information. The first screen explains why this is happening; the second collects the details. This reduces anxiety around sharing an SSN. Keeping verification and payment as separate actions also gives users a moment to review everything before the money goes out.
Screen 1 — Context
What this screen does
  • Conveys this is routine — not a fraud alert or exception
  • Provides transparency and removes blame
  • Gives personal benefit beyond compliance
  • Informs what details are required and what happens next
  • Gives control and offers an alternative
Screen 2 — Form
What this screen does
  • Leads with collaboration — "let's"
  • Affirms it's the last step before sending
  • Provides the format required for each field
  • Directly addresses the main concern about SSN storage

Challenge 02

Communicating a Merchant Account Limitation

Some transactions on a merchant's account have been flagged for review. The account has been temporarily limited — withdrawals are capped at 25% of the total balance until the merchant provides documentation. The challenge: communicate this clearly without losing the merchant's trust or their business.

Strategy
→
Be direct and clear about the temporary limitation
→
Explain why in simple, specific terms — not vague "security reasons"
→
Outline exactly what is needed and how to submit it
→
Provide a clear timeline for resolution
→
Reassure the merchant about what they can still do
→
Maintain a supportive, empathetic tone throughout
Email was chosen as the primary channel because merchants need to reference this information multiple times while gathering documents. Email also feels less jarring than an in-app block appearing without context.
Primary communication — Email
Subject: Restore full access to your account
To: [Merchant name]

Hi [Name],

We had to temporarily limit withdrawals from your account to 25% of your balance. We know this affects your business and we want to resolve this as quickly as possible.

Why this happened
  • [placeholder — transaction detail]
  • [placeholder — transaction detail]
To quickly resolve this, please share:
  • [placeholder — document required]
  • [placeholder — document required]
Next steps

You can easily upload your documents by clicking here, or visiting the profile section on the app.

Once we receive your documents, we'll review them in 2–3 business days and restore full access.

What you can still do

You can continue accepting payments and making sales. Only withdrawals above 25% are limited for now.

If you have any questions or need assistance, call us anytime at [phone number].

Challenge 03

Error Messages & Notifications

Three pieces of live copy — an error state, a 404 message, and a feature announcement — each with a different problem. The task was to rewrite them so users either get back on track, or move forward with confidence.

OriginalRewriteApproach
Loan application request denied. System maintenance window or internal error. We couldn't process your application, but it's nothing you did. Our system needs a minute. Clearly points toward the issue, conveys it's temporary, and takes accountability — in the humour we're all familiar with. It's not you, it's me.
Oops! This is embarrassing – we can't find the page you're looking for. We promise we'll get it right next time! Hmm, this page is missing…

It might have moved or the link's old. Don't worry, we'll help you find what you need!		 Casual yet thoughtful. Instead of promising to do better "next time," this affirms that help is available right now.
A new feature is available. Buy and sell crypto with PayPal. Crypto's here!

You can now buy Bitcoin and Ethereum on PayPal. Starts at a dollar.		 Less of a sales pitch, but exciting enough to look at. The "$1" entry point immediately removes the intimidation of crypto.
