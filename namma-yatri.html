Voice & Tone Map

Namma Yatri is built on three principles. Before rewriting any copy, I mapped each principle to vocabulary, verbosity, and grammar rules — so every change has a clear rationale.

🤝 Inclusive Rides for every rider 🛡️ Reliable Rides you can trust ✦ Simple Move around with ease
Vocabulary for all together we us let's on time safe secure fast save time save money easy quick direct respectful
Verbiosity Avoid metaphors, idioms, sarcasm Enough words for accurate information Enough words for unambiguous information
Grammar Lead with action · Active voice Lead with action · Active voice Lead with action · Active voice
Part 2 of 2

Copy Rewrites

Seven screens from the live app, rewritten against the voice map. Each numbered dot on the screenshot matches an annotation card.

01 — Destination Search
1
First field label
WasStarting from?
NowPickup
An awkward fragment, inconsistent with the second field. Pickup is the standard ride-booking term — it also echoes downstream messaging like "arrived at your pickup."
2
Second field label
WasWhere are you going?
NowDestination
Destination pairs with Pickup — consistent phrasing that echoes how the app talks about rides throughout the entire booking flow.
3
Saved place shortcut
WasHome
NowAdd Home
"Home" reads like a saved place, not an action — confusing next to "Add Work." Add Home makes both shortcuts clearly actions, consistent with each other.
02 — Location Not Found
1
Header
WasLocation not found
NowWe couldn't find that location.
Abrupt and system-like. Using "we" immediately aligns with the conversational tone used across the rest of the app.
2
Body + next step
WasWe couldnt find any matches for your search.
NowTry again or use the map to drop a pin.
Fixed the missing apostrophe. More critically — this gives the user two clear paths forward instead of a dead end with no recovery.
03 — GPS Off
1
Header
WasLooks like your GPS is turned off
NowLooks like your location is turned off
"GPS" is a backend term. Users see "Location" on their phone. One word swap, correct mental model.
2
Body
WasAllow us to get your exact location for smooth booking experience
NowWith location access, we'll set your pickup for you and get you moving.
The original feels coercive, like location is mandatory when it isn't. The rewrite leads with the benefit, and fixes the missing "a" before "smooth."
3
Button label
WasTurn on your GPS
NowTurn on location
Consistent with the header rewrite. Matches what users actually see when they navigate to device settings.
04 — Namma Yatri for Business
1
Header
WasNamma Yatri for Business is here!
NowIntroducing Namma Yatri for Business
"Introducing" signals a new feature clearly and confidently — without the hype of an exclamation mark.
2
Bullet 1
WasSeparate ride history and invoices...to ensure reconciliation is easy.
NowSeparate your personal and business rides and invoices, to easily track and claim expenses.
Removes finance jargon ("reconciliation") and reframes around what the user actually does — track and claim.
3
Bullet 2
WasSave time with automatic receipt uploads via expense integrations.
NowSave time with automatic receipt uploads.
Drops "expense integrations" — insider jargon that adds no clarity to the everyday user. The benefit stands without it.
4
Bullet 3
WasEnjoy travel benefits based on exclusive partnerships
NowEnjoy travel benefits through our partner companies.
More concrete and warmer. "Our partner companies" feels like the app is sharing something with you, not marketing at you.
05 — Work Email
1
Header
WasWhat's your company email?
NowWhat's your work email address?
"Work email" is neutral — covers both employees and business owners, and doesn't clash with the "for Business" feature name above.
2
Body
WasWe'll send you an email that'll verify you right away.
NowWe'll send you an email to verify right away.
Cleaner phrasing. The original used "email" to mean both the address and the message being sent — ambiguous on the same screen.
3
Input placeholder
WasEnter your business email ID
NowEnter your work email address
Consistent with the header. "Email address" is plainer than "email ID" — avoids mixing "company" and "business" on the same screen.
06 — Unexpected Events Check
1
Body text
WasEnable unexpected events check to receive alerts when your route deviates from the planned one.
NowWe'll send you an alert if your driver takes a different route than planned.
The original repeats the feature name right after the header and toggle label already say it. "Your driver takes a different route" is exactly how a rider thinks about this.
📌
Design note: The header and toggle label both read "Unexpected events check" — identical copy in two adjacent elements. Worth flagging to the product designer as a hierarchy issue.
07 — Post Ride Check
1
Body text
WasGet check in notifications once your ride is complete. Get automated calls in case of no-response
NowWe'll send you a check-in notification once your ride is complete. If there's no response, you'll receive an automated call from us.
Passive "Get check in notifications" reads like a system log. Active voice makes it feel like the app is looking out for you. The escalation — notification first, then call — is now clearly sequenced.
